# Platform Economics

I design economic and architectural systems that turn large-scale IT portfolios into growth capital.

This repository documents frameworks, principles, and strategies for making high-stakes platform decisions under uncertainty — across cloud, hybrid, on-prem, SaaS, and AI environments.

---

## Executive Summary

Most organizations focus on **cutting costs**.  
The real challenge is deciding:
- **which costs can safely be reduced**
- **which investments unlock future capacity**
- **how architectural choices drive long-term economic outcomes**

> Architecture is an economic decision long before it becomes a technical one.

Platform economics is about **capital allocation**, not cost reduction.  
It integrates governance, decision authority, and cross-functional context to enable sustainable efficiency, optionality, and growth.

---

## Core Principles

- **Architecture Shapes Economics** – Design choices dictate scalability, flexibility, and unit economics before spend occurs.  
- **Capital Allocation Over Cost Cutting** – Decisions prioritize business outcomes, not immediate savings.  
- **Decision-Making Under Uncertainty** – Optimization without context creates risk; trade-offs must be explicit.  
- **Systems Over Tactics** – Sustainable efficiency emerges from governance, incentives, and defaults, not one-off dashboards.
